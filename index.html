Friedrich Nietzsche

Father of Nihilism

Timeline of Nietzsche's Life:

  • 1844 - Born in Röcken, Saxony, Prussia, German Confederation
  • 1854 - Attended Domgymnasium in Naumburg.
  • 1858 - Studied at Schulprofta.
  • 1864 - Graduated at Schulprofta and started studying theology and classical philosophy at the University of Bonn.
  • 1865 - Writes a letter to his sister, Elisabeth, who was deeply religious, concerning his loss of faith.
  • 1867 - Signed up for one year of voluntary service with the Prussian artillery division in Naumburg, where he was regarded as one of the finest riders among his fellow recruits. However in 1868 while jumping into the saddle, tore two muscles and was unable to walk for months, deciding to return to studies.
  • 1869 - Became a professor of classic philology at the University of Basel and was awarded honorary doctorate by Leipzig University.
  • 1870 - Served in Franco-Prussian War
  • 1873 - Nietzsche began to accumulate several notes, which shared shared the orientation of a cultural critique, challenging the developing German culture suggested by Schopenhauer and Wagner
  • 1879 - Due to declining health, Nietzsche became an independent philosopher, traveling often to find climates more conductive to his health
  • 1883 - Tried and failed to obtain a lecturing post at the University of Leipzig
  • 1886 - Broke with his publisher Ernst Schmeitzner, disgusted by his anti-semitic opinions.
  • 1889 - Suffered a mental breakdown and started going insane, commanding German emperor to go to Rome to be shot.
  • 1900 - Died in Weimar, Saxe-Weimar-Eisenach, German Empire
